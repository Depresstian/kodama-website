Compound Training vs Isolation Training
>There is a foundational difference in the target of exercises that changes how they should be implemented
>Compound exercises feature more movement and engage are larger set of muscles, typically a muscle group
>Isolation exercises focus on engaging a specific set of muscles for greater effect
>I prefer compound exercises which ensure a more balanced gain of strength

Coming up with a workout plan

Kettlebells

I like kettlebells for a few reasons.
>1. First, kettlebell exercises normally engage whole muscle groups and suit my preference for compound training.
>2. Second, they're a single isolated piece of equipment that isn't really a hassle to store but can give a great workout
>3. Finally, I had access to it. The kettlebell swing was one of my first workout breakthroughs only because I had one available. As such, I've come to see it as the old relaible option.
